Technical Mentoring

A case for investing in external mentoring for your engineering team

Why Invest in External Mentoring

Hiring an external mentor is a multiplier on the investment you have already made in your engineering team.

Break Past Local Optima

Teams converge on what works and get stuck there - shared context turns blind spots into consensus. An external mentor who has seen how other companies, scales, and domains solve the same problems can challenge assumptions that no one on the inside thinks to question.

Tailored, Not Generic

Online courses are generic and most never get completed. Mentoring starts from where the person actually is - their specific gaps, their codebase, their career context - and adapts as they grow. A live conversation, not a recording, with real follow-up across sessions.

Compress the Learning Curve

Some lessons take years to learn through experience alone - navigating architectural trade-offs, thinking in production, building technical judgement. A mentor who has already made those mistakes can help engineers skip the slow path and reach senior-level thinking faster.

Invest in Retention

Engineers who feel they are growing stay. Engineers who feel stuck leave. Dedicated mentoring is one of the strongest signals a company can send that it takes individual development seriously - and it costs a fraction of a single backfill.

What the Mentoring Covers

Mentoring is tailored to the individual, but typically falls into one or more of the following areas:

Technical Excellence

Deep, hands-on guidance on the hard software engineering challenges - the kind that only surface in production and rarely get covered in courses or books

  • Distributed systems design, architecture trade-offs
  • Java/JVM best practices, multithreading, concurrency, and performance
  • AI adoption mindset - pragmatic, production-focused guidance on integrating LLMs and AI tooling into existing systems
  • Production readiness, operational thinking, and incident response

Career Growth & Visibility

Engineering skill alone doesn't build a career. Mentoring in this area helps engineers articulate their value, build a professional reputation, and navigate the business side of technology.

  • Building a personal brand and professional visibility in the industry
  • Communicating technical value to non-technical stakeholders
  • Selling ideas, proposals, and architectural decisions internally
  • Navigating career transitions - IC to lead, employee to consultant, or into independent work

Public Speaking & Workshops

Practical coaching on crafting and delivering technical content that resonates with developer audiences.

  • Developing conference talk proposals (CFPs) that get accepted
  • Structuring and delivering engaging technical presentations
  • Designing and running hands-on workshops - from exercises to pacing
  • Building a speaking track record and navigating the conference circuit

How It Works

  • Regular sessions (remote), tailored to the mentee's context and goals
  • Ongoing engagement that builds over time - not a one-off workshop
  • Adapts to individual contributors or small teams
  • Starts with a free 20-minute intro call to assess fit

Low Risk, Easy to Try

There is no long-term contract or upfront commitment. Start with a few sessions and see the impact first-hand. If it is not the right fit, simply stop - no lock-in, no questions asked.
