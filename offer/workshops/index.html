Workshops

Hands-on technical training for engineering teams. Not a documentation walkthrough - participants get production war stories, OSS internals, and pragmatic advice that works in real systems.

What Makes These Different

Anyone can read the docs to you. I don't do that. Every workshop is built on years of building and running production systems, contributing to open-source projects, and working with teams across the industry. You get the context that documentation can't give you.

Production war stories. Real incidents, real trade-offs, real design decisions. I share what went wrong, what worked, and what I'd do differently - so you don't have to learn these lessons the hard way.

Pragmatic advice for real systems. Not textbook answers - guidance that holds up under load, at scale, and on a deadline. What actually matters in production and what you can safely skip.

Industry insights. Patterns I've seen work (and fail) across different teams, companies, and domains. The kind of perspective you only get from being deep in the ecosystem.

Internals and OSS trivia. I dig into how things actually work under the hood - JDK internals, framework source code, open-source implementation details. Understanding the "why" behind the API makes you a better engineer.

Why a Live Workshop

You can ask "what about our case?"

Self-study courses are generic by design. A live workshop lets your team ask questions about their actual codebase, their architecture, their problems - and get answers on the spot.

Completion rates that actually work

Most online courses are never finished. A focused, scheduled workshop with a live instructor means your team shows up, stays engaged, and walks out with real skills - not a half-watched playlist.

The whole team levels up together

Instead of individuals learning different things at different times, everyone builds the same shared understanding. That pays off immediately in code reviews, design discussions, and day-to-day decisions.

Feedback loops are instant

When someone's confused or heading down the wrong path, I see it in real time and correct course. No waiting for a forum reply or re-watching a video hoping for clarity.

How I Run Workshops

01

Live coding, not pre-baked demos

I build most things from scratch right in front of participants. You see the thinking process, the mistakes, the debugging - not a polished copy-paste from a prepared repo.

02

Code-first, not slides-first

Participants work on a real project with prepared exercises and tests. Slides are there to set context - the IDE is where the real workshop happens.

03

Problems before solutions

I let participants experience the problem firsthand before introducing the pattern or technique that solves it. Understanding the "why" sticks longer than memorizing the "how".

04

Tailored to your team

Every workshop is adapted to the group's experience level. I adjust pace, depth, and examples on the fly based on what I see in the room.

05

Questions welcome anytime

I don't save Q&A for the end. Participants interrupt, ask, and challenge in real time - that's often where the best discussions happen.

06

Follow-up call included

A few weeks after the workshop, we meet for a consulting call to go through questions that came up once your team started applying the ideas in their own codebase.

07

AI-native option available

Any workshop can be run with AI coding assistants as a first-class tool. Participants learn to use AI effectively for the topic at hand - not as a gimmick, but as part of their real workflow.
