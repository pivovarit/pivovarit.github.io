01 — Consulting

Engineering Transformation at a FinTech Scale-up

Series B · 80-person engineering org · 6-month engagement

consulting leadership culture mentoring

The Challenge

A Series B payment infrastructure company had scaled from 15 to 80 engineers in 18 months. Hiring speed had outpaced culture. Teams operated in silos, code reviews were inconsistent, architectural decisions were made ad-hoc, and two senior engineers had already resigned citing "chaos." A third was quietly interviewing.

The Approach

  • Weeks 1–2: Embedded observation — attended standups, architecture discussions, and ran 40+ one-on-ones across all engineering levels.
  • Weeks 3–4: Identified three root causes: lack of shared standards, no structured growth paths, and architectural decisions bypassing the people closest to the code.
  • Month 2: Facilitated a collaboratively authored Engineering Principles document — written by engineers, not handed down by management.
  • Month 3: Introduced a lightweight RFC process for architectural decisions, replacing ad-hoc Slack threads.
  • Months 4–5: Designed and launched an internal mentoring program with explicit promotion criteria and regular check-ins.
  • Month 6: Defined and helped hire an Engineering Excellence Lead from within — someone who would own this work after the engagement ended.

The Outcome

Three engineers were promoted within 90 days of the program launch. Feature delivery accelerated as teams stopped waiting for informal permission to make decisions. The RFC process eliminated late-stage architectural rework. The engagement ended with a permanent internal owner in place.

02 — Performance Engineering

JVM Performance Overhaul for an E-Commerce Platform

E-Commerce · €500M GMV · 10-week engagement

performance JVM concurrency architecture

The Challenge

A high-traffic e-commerce platform was losing an estimated €15k/hour during peak sales events due to P99 latency spikes in the order processing service. The internal team had applied standard fixes — read replicas, horizontal scaling, Redis caching — but improvements plateaued after three attempts. They needed someone to find the actual bottleneck.

The Approach

  • Week 1: Instrumentation first — added fine-grained Micrometer metrics and async-profiler sampling. Generated Flame Graphs from peak-load production captures before touching any code.
  • Week 2: Identified three root causes: a lock-convoy in the inventory reservation service, 2.4 GB/min object allocation in the pricing engine causing GC pressure, and a thundering herd problem in the session cache layer.
  • Weeks 3–5: Incremental fixes — replaced the coarse lock with a striped approach, rewrote the pricing engine's inner loop using primitive arrays to eliminate boxing overhead, added request coalescing to the session layer.
  • Weeks 6–7: JVM tuning — migrated from G1GC to ZGC, right-sized heap regions based on profiled allocation behavior, bounded thread pool sizes to observed parallelism limits.
  • Weeks 8–10: Load testing at 3× peak, incremental production rollout, runbook documentation, and a performance review process to prevent regression.

The Outcome

P99 latency dropped from 2,400ms to 850ms under equivalent load. The system's behavior under peak became predictable — no spikes, no timeout cascades. Three overprovisioned EC2 instance types were decommissioned. The following Black Friday was the first in three years with zero performance-related incidents.
